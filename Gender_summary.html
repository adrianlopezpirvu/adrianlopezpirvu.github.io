Gender Disparity in Crime Rates: Men Still Committing More Offenses, but Trend Shows Improvement

By Juan Santos, Adrián López 13:00 PM ET

In this graph, we embark on a journey through the fluctuating tide of arrests, examining the ebb and flow of law enforcement's interaction with society, divided by gender.

For men, the trajectory is marked by peaks and valleys. From 2005 to 2010, the number of arrests steadily rose, reaching a zenith of 340 thousand in a single year. This plateau persisted into 2011 before descending steadily, hitting a nadir of 113 thousand by 2020. However, a resurgence followed, with arrests climbing back up to 152 thousand by 2022.

Contrastingly, the graph for women paints a picture of relative stability. Starting at 55 thousand in the early years, the number of female arrests remains relatively constant, with a slight decline over time to 32 thousand by the end of the period. The lowest point was recorded in 2020, with 22 thousand arrests.

Despite their disparate trajectories, a striking comparison emerges in 2022. The difference in arrests between men and women stands at 120 thousand, a significant decrease from the initial gap of 245 thousand at the beginning of the period. This shift underscores not only changes in law enforcement practices but also potentially evolving societal dynamics influencing crime rates among different genders.
