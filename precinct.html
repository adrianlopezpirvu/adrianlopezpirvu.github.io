Density of Violent Incidents by Precinct and Year in New York City

By Juan Santos, Adrián López 12:00 PM ET

This map, a window into the pulsating soul of the city, unfolds the evolution of violent arrests or those related to weapons in each precinct from 2017 to 2022. Like a silent spectator, it allows us to observe how the shadows of crime shift through the neighborhoods, painting a landscape that changes over time.

Throughout these turbulent five years, the lines that delineate the precincts have remained steadfast, like vigilant guardians of the law. However, behind these lines, the numbers dance to the rhythm of crime. The 2020 pandemic, an event that froze the world in a state of collective anxiety, left its mark on arrest statistics. The streets, once vibrant with urban life, grew quieter, and arrest numbers declined, as if the virus itself had imposed a curfew.

But, like the sun emerging after a storm, life in the city of New York resumed, and with it, criminal activity found its rhythm once again. The following years saw a resurgence in arrests, a trend that defies even the most daunting events.

Amidst the darkest shadows of this narrative, glimmers of hope emerge. Precincts 111, 112, and 94, nestled in the urban fabric, stand out for their relative calm amid chaos. Despite being surrounded by neighborhoods with more notorious criminal activity, these precincts stand as oases of tranquility, where law and order reign with firm determination.

In contrast, precincts 75, 40, and 44 emerge as the brightest stars in the crime firmament. With a higher frequency of arrests than others, these neighborhoods challenge stability, defying law enforcement with a persistence that defies any attempt at easy containment.

Thus, this map, more than a mere representation of data, becomes a narrative of the constant struggle between good and evil, between light and darkness, that defines life in the city that never sleeps.
