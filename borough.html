Distribution of Arrests by Borough and Race in New York City in 2020

By Juan Santos, Adrián López 12:00 PM ET

In the following plot, seamlessly continuing from the previous narrative, the five major boroughs of New York City are depicted, along with arrests over the past five months categorized by race. This plot serves as a highly illustrative tool, and it is recommended to leverage its interactivity by selecting plots for one race at a time to gain a clear understanding of how crimes are distributed across the city.

For individuals identified as white, there is a distinct concentration in the southern area of Manhattan and the southwestern region of Brooklyn, with additional crimes occurring more centrally within the city.

For individuals identified as black, five geographically distinct clusters can be observed: again, in the southern part of Manhattan, the intersection area between the Bronx and Manhattan, and the central and southern regions of Queens.

For those identified as white Hispanic, crimes appear more dispersed, following a similar trend to those previously described. However, it is worth noting the northern area of Queens, which had not yet been mentioned.

For individuals identified as Asian/Pacific Islander, crimes are most concentrated in small clusters. Notable areas include the southern tip of Manhattan, the western part of Brooklyn, and the central and northern regions of Queens.

As for individuals identified as American Indian/Alaskan Native, drawing conclusions proves challenging due to the limited number of crimes, and no significant concentrations are discernible.
